# Source Comparability Rules

## A Grade: Strictly Comparable

A source pair is A grade when all of the following are satisfied:

- same trial
- same treatment arm
- same dose/regimen
- same safety analysis population
- data cutoff dates identical or within 30 days
- AE definition and grade category aligned
- denominator difference absent or fully explained

A-grade pairs enter the primary analysis.

## B Grade: Partially Comparable

A source pair is B grade when the materials are broadly aligned but have one or more limited differences:

- data cutoff dates differ by 31 to 180 days
- safety population denominator differs by no more than 5%
- updated publication or longer follow-up uses otherwise comparable definitions
- minor terminology differences can be mapped to the same predefined safety concept

B-grade pairs enter sensitivity analyses only.

## C Grade: Not Directly Comparable

A source pair is C grade when direct numeric comparison would be misleading:

- different dose groups are merged or split
- monotherapy and combination therapy are mixed
- treatment-related and all-cause AE cannot be separated
- safety population differs substantially
- data cutoff differs by more than 180 days
- long-term updated publication cannot be aligned to the approval evidence package

C-grade pairs are summarized descriptively and excluded from omission-rate and numeric-discordance calculations.
